Design Overview

What is Kid Science?

Kid Science is an aesthetic surrounding educational efforts to make science appealing to children through presenting the field as fun, accessible, and wonder-filled, using kooky characters, bright colors, highly stylized images, and doable experiments. It draws heavily from the visual language of 1990s and 2000s children's educational television, science museums, and picture books.

The design philosophy centers on transforming complex scientific concepts into approachable, exciting visual experiences -- making microscopes feel like treasure hunts and chemistry sets feel like magic kits.

Design Principles

The guiding rules behind every Kid Science design decision.

🎯

Accessibility Over Accuracy

Simplify complex concepts into bold, clear visuals; stylization is preferred over photorealism.

🤩

Curiosity and Wonder

Every design element should provoke a "Wow!" or "How does that work?" reaction.

🤚

Hands-On Engagement

Layouts should feel interactive and inviting, as if the viewer could reach in and participate.

🔥

Enthusiastic Energy

Nothing is subdued; colors are bright, type is bold, characters are animated with excitement.

🌿

Respect for Nature

Nature is presented as fascinating and worthy of study, not scary or clinical.

👋

Direct Address

Content speaks directly to the child viewer with warmth and encouragement.

🎨

Organized Chaos

Designs are busy and packed with information but structured enough to navigate easily.

🧪

Experiential Learning

Visual emphasis on doing, observing, and discovering rather than passive reading.

Visual Identity

Color Palette

A high-saturation, high-contrast palette for maximum visual impact and child engagement. No pastels allowed -- colors are bold and confident.

🌿

Science Greens

Primary accent for nature, biology, and growth. The dominant color of the palette alongside orange.

🌞

Energy Oranges

Secondary accent for excitement, call-to-action, dinosaur themes, and discovery moments.

🪨

Earth Browns

Grounding neutral instead of gray. Warmth and earthiness for fossils, soil, and natural materials.

Green-orange dualism is the heart of Kid Science: green represents nature, biology, and growth, while orange represents energy, excitement, and discovery. They are the dominant pair that gives this aesthetic its signature look.

The natural world is full of amazing surprises waiting to be discovered. From the tiniest bacteria to the largest blue whale, every living thing has a story to tell. Scientists use observation, experimentation, and curiosity to unlock these stories -- and you can too!
Accessible Body -- Lexend (400)
Lexend is specifically optimized for reading comfort and accessibility, making it perfect for longer scientific explanations where young readers need every advantage. Its generous letter-spacing and tall x-height reduce cognitive load.

Pairing recommendation: Use Fredoka or Baloo 2 for headlines with Nunito Sans or Lexend for body text. Add Patrick Hand for handwritten annotation elements and Bangers for high-energy impact labels.

Hands-On Learning

The Classic Volcano Experiment

A step-by-step Kid Science experiment layout -- the quintessential DIY project for young scientists everywhere.

Bio
Chem
Phys
Aqua
Magic
1

Gather Your Materials

You'll need: baking soda, vinegar, dish soap, food coloring, a plastic bottle, and clay or paper-mache to build your volcano shape.

2

Build Your Volcano

Shape clay or paper-mache around the plastic bottle to form a mountain. Leave the bottle opening at the top. Let it dry completely and paint it brown and gray!

3

Mix the Secret Formula

Add 2 tablespoons of baking soda into the bottle. Squeeze in some dish soap and a few drops of red and orange food coloring.

4

ERUPTION TIME!

Pour vinegar into the bottle and step back! The acid-base reaction creates carbon dioxide gas, producing a fizzy, foamy "lava" eruption!

Always do experiments with a grown-up nearby! Vinegar can sting your eyes, so wear your safety goggles. And do this one outside or on a tray -- it gets messy (that's the fun part!).

Structure

Layout Principles

Backgrounds

Bright, Clean Canvases

White or cream backgrounds with subtle textures -- notebook lines, graph paper, chalkboard -- that evoke educational environments.

Layout

Packed But Organized

Content-dense like a science poster or museum exhibit panel, but structured with clear visual hierarchy and generous padding inside elements.

Containers

Bold, Rounded Cards

Cards with generous border-radius (12-20px), thick colored borders, and soft shadows. Fact cards in 2x2 or 3-column grids.

Dividers

Bold Color Bands

Sections separated by thick colored bars, zigzag waves, or rainbow gradients in accent colors rather than subtle dividers.

"Every section should feel like a new exhibit at the science museum!"

🔗 Related Aesthetics 🔗
Connections

Related Aesthetics

How Kid Science connects to and differs from neighboring design families.

Closest Sibling

Kidcore

Shares bright colors, playful energy, and child-oriented visual language, but Kidcore is broader (toys, games, cartoons) while Kid Science focuses specifically on scientific education.

Grown-Up Version

Science Academia

Shares laboratory motifs and scientific iconography but with muted colors, serif fonts, and institutional formality rather than playful exuberance.

Educational Optimism

Utopian Scholastic

Shares the educational optimism and belief in learning as empowerment; Kid Science is its science-specific subset.

Clinical Cousin

Med-Tech Visions

Shares some lab/medical imagery (goggles, lab coats, microscopes) but Med-Tech is clinical and futuristic rather than playful and child-oriented.

Kid Science

Where microscopes become treasure hunts and chemistry sets become magic kits. Every child is a scientist -- they just need the right invitation.
