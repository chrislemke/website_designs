The Feeling of Being by the Sea

Coastal Style draws inspiration from shoreline environments -- the Hamptons, Western France, and the Mediterranean coast. It emphasizes natural light, open spaces, and relaxed elegance, with a palette pulled directly from the shore: sky, sea, sand, driftwood, and weathered stone.

The approach is casual yet polished, favoring soft, diffused visual weight over hard edges and heavy shadows. Indoor-outdoor flow, layered neutrals with blue accents, and an emphasis on natural materials define its character.

This page is both a reference for and a demonstration of the Coastal Style aesthetic.

Guiding Philosophy

Design Principles

01

Light & Airy Composition

Abundant whitespace evoking open skies and wide beaches. Let content breathe.

02

Casual, Relaxed Elegance

Polished but never stiff or overly formal. Approachable sophistication.

03

Indoor-Outdoor Flow

Blurred boundaries between content sections, seamless transitions throughout.

04

Layered Neutrals with Blue Accents

A restrained base palette punctuated by ocean tones for anchoring.

05

Soft, Diffused Visual Weight

Avoid hard edges and heavy shadows in favor of gentle gradients and subtle depth.

06

Natural Material Emphasis

Textures and patterns drawn from organic, coastal materials ground the design.

Structure & Space

Layout Principles

Open layouts with generous padding, asymmetric balance, full-width heroes, rounded cards, and wave-shaped section dividers.

Open & Spacious

Generous padding and margin throughout. Let content breathe like open skies and wide beaches.

Wide Containers

Max-width of 1100--1400px with ample side margins for comfortable, expansive reading.

Card-Based Content

Rounded-corner cards with soft shadows for grouping. Like smooth pebbles on the shore.

Wave Dividers

SVG curves and clip-paths separate content blocks organically, echoing the shoreline.

Asymmetric Balance

Natural, organic placement rather than rigid symmetry. Horizontal flow like a shoreline.

Full-Width Heroes

Large gradient washes spanning the viewport. Hierarchy through weight and color, not density.

Variations

Sub-styles & Variations

Coastal Style branches into distinct regional and tonal interpretations.

Classic

Hamptons Style

Expansive, luminous, and comfortable. Crisp white and navy blue dominate. Tailored, preppy refinement with ticking stripes and premium natural materials.

Warm

Mediterranean Coastal

Lagoon colors meet ochre and terracotta. Arched shapes, botanical motifs, and wrought-iron accents evoke the sun-drenched shores of Southern Europe.

Contemporary

Modern Coastal

California-casual with clean lines. Avoids overt beach themes. Neutral-dominant with subtle blue accents, woven textures, and generous whitespace.
