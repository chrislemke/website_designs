Also Known As: 70s Ultra-Modern

The Future as Dreamed in the Space Age

Supergraphic Ultramodern succeeds Raygun Gothic, Space Age, Googie, and Mid-Century Modern. The name combines "supergraphics" -- oversized, wall-spanning graphic elements in vibrant colors and geometric shapes -- with "ultramodern," referencing the cutting-edge futuristic atmosphere the style aimed to evoke.

The mood is optimistic, playful, and technologically confident -- a vision of the future rendered in tangible, consumer-friendly form. Every surface becomes a canvas. Every shape curves toward tomorrow.

Core Visual Traits

The building blocks of the Supergraphic Ultramodern aesthetic -- each trait defines how surfaces, forms, and spaces are treated.

Oversized Supergraphics

Massive geometric shapes, stripes, arcs, and color fields painted or applied across walls, ceilings, floors, and furniture, treating surfaces as immersive canvases.

Curves and Rounded Forms

Bulbous furniture silhouettes, sweeping arcs, rounded corners, pod-like enclosures -- straight lines are rare. Everything flows.

Rainbow Color Application

Full-spectrum rainbow gradients used as decorative bands, arcs, and stripe sequences across large surfaces. Color deployed at scale.

Flat Graphic Iconography

Simplified, two-dimensional symbols and pictograms. Bold, reductive visual communication distilled to essential forms.

White-Dominated Spaces

Pristine white backgrounds punctuated by vivid orange, rainbow, or earth-tone accents. The white void is the futuristic canvas.

Glossy, Reflective Surfaces

Plastics, fiberglass, shiny metals, and lacquered finishes creating a sleek, futuristic sheen. Every surface catches light.

Inflatable and Modular Forms

Soft, pneumatic shapes suggesting comfort and playfulness. Modular, reconfigurable seating and furniture as living sculpture.

Op Art-Influenced Patterns

Optical illusion grids, concentric circles, and interference patterns adding visual dynamism at architectural scale.

Environmental Immersion

Graphics and color flow across every surface, erasing the boundary between decoration and architecture. Space becomes experience.

Palette Usage Guidelines

White as Canvas

Use white as the dominant surface color -- it is the canvas onto which supergraphics are placed.

Orange is the Hero

Deploy orange as the primary accent; it is the single most characteristic color of the era.

Rainbow Continuity

Rainbow sequences should flow as continuous bands, not as isolated color spots. Let color travel.

Vivid Saturation

Avoid desaturated or pastel treatments -- colors are vivid and saturated. Large color fields are preferred.

Supergraphic Ultramodern typography is dominated by Helvetica and its geometric sans-serif relatives. Type is clean, confident, and functional -- a direct expression of International Typographic Style principles applied to futuristic environments. Bold, heavy weights for headlines. All-caps display text with generous letter-spacing for signage and environmental graphics.

Typography Rules

Headlines

Bold geometric sans-serif, often ALL CAPS, generous letter-spacing (0.08-0.15em). Dramatic scale.

Body Text

Clean sans-serif, regular weight, comfortable line-height (1.6-1.8). Restrained and functional.

Scale Contrast

Massive headlines against restrained body text. The jump in size should be dramatic -- not subtle.

Typeface Limit

Limit to 1-2 typeface families. Typographic restraint is a core principle. Icons complement type.

Design Principles

The guiding rules for authentic Supergraphic Ultramodern expression.

  1. 01 Treat every surface as a potential canvas for bold graphic expression
  2. 02 Use scale as a primary tool -- oversized graphics command attention and transform spatial perception
  3. 03 Balance white minimalist space with concentrated bursts of vibrant color
  4. 04 Embrace curves, organic forms, and soft shapes over angular geometry
  5. 05 Materials should project futurism: glossy plastics, chrome, fiberglass over wood or stone
  6. 06 Flat, reductive iconography over detailed illustration
  7. 07 Create immersive, enveloping environments rather than decorated surfaces
  8. 08 Celebrate technological optimism and consumer playfulness

Layout Principles

How space, structure, and composition are organized in the Supergraphic Ultramodern environment.

Curved, Flowing Compositions

Layouts follow arcs and organic paths rather than rigid rectangular grids. Movement is implied in every arrangement.

White Space as Canvas

Generous white backgrounds establish the pristine futuristic environment. Emptiness is intentional and powerful.

Oversized Graphic Elements

Key visual elements span the full width or height of the viewport. Scale is a design tool, not an afterthought.

Immersive Color Sections

Full-bleed color blocks create distinct environmental zones. Each section is its own color world.

Asymmetric Placement

Elements positioned with confident off-center balance. Dynamic composition over rigid symmetry.

Rounded Containers

Content blocks use rounded corners, pill shapes, and circular forms. The pod is the fundamental container.

Design Guidelines

Essential rules for authentic Supergraphic Ultramodern execution.

Do

  • Use white as the dominant background, treating it as a futuristic void
  • Deploy oversized graphic elements at environmental scale
  • Make orange the hero accent color -- it is the signature hue
  • Use rainbow stripe sequences as structural dividers and decorative bands
  • Apply generous rounded corners to all containers (2rem+ border-radius)
  • Keep typography in the Helvetica / neo-grotesque sans-serif family
  • Use flat, simplified iconography and pictograms
  • Create dramatic scale contrast between headlines and body text
  • Let curves dominate -- arcs, circles, and organic shapes define the aesthetic

Don't

  • Use angular, sharp-cornered containers (contradicts the curvilinear language)
  • Apply detailed textures, woodgrain, or natural materials (the aesthetic is synthetic)
  • Use serif or decorative typefaces (Helvetica-style neutrality is essential)
  • Overcrowd layouts -- generous white space is the canvas for supergraphics
  • Use small, timid accent colors -- bold, oversized color application is the point
  • Apply drop shadows or 3D effects (depth comes from overlapping flat planes)
  • Mix too many competing graphic motifs per section
  • Use pastel or desaturated colors -- the palette is vivid and confident

Related Aesthetics

The design family tree -- predecessors, siblings, and descendants of the Supergraphic Ultramodern movement.

Mid-Century Modern

Direct predecessor; Supergraphic Ultramodern inherits its curved forms but amplifies the color and graphic scale.

Space Age

Overlapping sibling; shares futuristic material language and pod-like forms.

Raygun Gothic

Earlier retro-futurist ancestor; Supergraphic Ultramodern is its 1970s evolution.

Earth Tones

Contemporary overlap; shares brown, orange, gold, and avocado palette.

Cassette Futurism

Overlapping aesthetic; shares the analog-futuristic material language of the late 70s.

Disco

Contemporary sibling; shares the reflective surfaces, chrome accents, and exuberant color.

International Typographic Style

Design foundation; provides the Helvetica-centric, grid-based typographic discipline.

Y2K Futurism

Direct descendant; the 1990s revival of Supergraphic Ultramodern's curves and optimism.

Memphis Design

Successor; replaced Ultramodern's smooth curves with jagged geometry and pattern.

Laser Grid

Successor aesthetic; shares the neon-bright palette but shifts to digital wireframe forms.

Groovival

Modern revival; contemporary reinterpretation of 70s curves, rainbows, and earth tones.

Flat Design

Modern digital descendant; shares the commitment to two-dimensional, bold-color graphic expression.
