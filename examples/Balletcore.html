Tall Display / Italiana
Prima Ballerina Assoluta

Italiana's tall, narrow letterforms mirror the elongated lines of a dancer's body in extension. Best used for display headings where verticality and poise define the composition.

Body / Libre Baskerville — Accent / Dancing Script

The body text carries the elegance of classical serif design with refined stroke contrast and generous x-height. It reads with the same quiet confidence as a dancer holding an arabesque — perfectly balanced, entirely composed.

plier, relever, tourner
Interface / Josefin Sans — Subheading / Tenor Sans
Navigation · Buttons · Labels · Captions

Tenor Sans offers clean classical proportions for subheadings and secondary text, bridging the gap between the decorative display and the functional interface layer.

The Practice

Design Principles

The daily discipline behind every graceful interface

  • Principle I Let every element breathe — generous white space is the silence between movements
  • Principle II Build depth through translucency rather than shadow, like tulle over warm skin
  • Principle III Choose elongated, light-weight typography that echoes the lines of a dancer in extension
  • Principle IV Animate with intention — smooth, controlled transitions that feel choreographed
  • Principle V Maintain a tonal palette; let values shift gently like light across a studio floor
  • Principle VI Balance softness with discipline — Balletcore is romantic, but never careless
