The Golden Age of Glamour & Cinematic Opulence

1920s — 1950s  •  Southern California

Explore

Hollywood Regency is a glamorous interior design and decorative arts aesthetic originating from 1920s–1950s Southern California and the Golden Age of Hollywood cinema. It is characterized by opulence, theatrical elegance, luxurious materials, metallic and mirrored accents, and extravagant asymmetrical balance.


The style was designed to convey cinematic glamour — spaces that make everything (and everyone) look stunning. It merges the ornamental drama of Baroque and Rococo with the geometric confidence of Art Deco, filtered through the lens of Old Hollywood sophistication.

Visual Identity

Core Motifs & Patterns

☼

Sunburst Motifs

Radiating fan or starburst shapes used in mirrors, clocks, and decorative elements — the single most iconic Hollywood Regency motif.

◢

Greek Key Patterns

Geometric meander borders used in trim, rugs, and frames — a classical motif conveying sophistication.

✿

Animal Prints

Zebra, leopard, cheetah, tiger, and snakeskin patterns used as bold accent textures.

◊

Chevron & Zig-Zag

Angular repetitions used in textiles, carpets, and wallcoverings for dynamic visual rhythm.

☶

Chinoiserie Elements

Asian-inspired decorative motifs: pagodas, dragons, bamboo lattice — exotic and alluring.

☘

Tropical Elements

Palm trees, hibiscus flowers, and exotic botanical motifs evoking Southern California glamour.

▩

Checkered Patterns

Classic black-and-white floor tiles or diamond arrangements — dramatic and graphic.

❀

Bold Florals

Oversized, dramatic floral patterns in both monochrome and vivid color palettes.

❖

Tufted Upholstery

Diamond-quilted surfaces with button tufting — a signature luxury texture.

⚛

Scrollwork

Rococo and Baroque-inspired ornamental curves, plasterwork, and decorative flourishes.

Philosophy

Design Principles

I

Maximalist Approach

Layered, rich, and visually abundant. Hollywood Regency embraces excess with purpose — never minimalist, always deliberately opulent.

II

Theatrical Presentation

Every element is staged for dramatic visual impact, as though lit by a cinematographer for a close-up.

III

Asymmetrical Balance

Intentional visual tension balanced through weight rather than mirror symmetry — dynamic yet harmonious.

IV

High-Contrast Pairings

Bold darks with brilliant lights, matte with gloss, rough with smooth — tension that creates visual electricity.

V

Curvaceous Forms

Rounded, sensuous shapes in furniture and layout elements that evoke softness within structure.

VI

Mirrored & Reflective Surfaces

Amplify light, create depth, and add glamour — reflective surfaces are essential to the Hollywood Regency vocabulary.

VII

Lacquered High-Gloss Finishes

Polished, light-catching surfaces throughout — wet-look lacquer that commands attention.

VIII

Scale & Proportion Play

Oversized mirrors, dramatic lighting, large-scale patterns — commanding spatial presence through deliberate scale shifts.

Spaces that make everything — and everyone — look stunning. Cinematic glamour filtered through gold, glass, and the unapologetic pursuit of beauty.

Context

Related Aesthetics

Art Deco

Close sibling; shares geometric motifs, gold metallics, and glamour but is more angular and geometric.

Baroque

Ancestor influence; contributes scrollwork, ornamental excess, and theatrical drama.

Rococo

Ancestor influence; contributes curves, playfulness, and decorative plasterwork.

Old Hollywood

Cultural twin; the lifestyle and fashion aesthetic that accompanies Hollywood Regency interiors.

Maximalism

Shared philosophy; both embrace abundance, layering, and visual richness over restraint.

Mid-Century Modern

Contemporary counterpart; emerged in the same era but favors simplicity over opulence.

Regency

Historical reference; the English Regency era (1811-1820) provides classical architectural forms.

Hellenic

Decorative influence; Greek key patterns and classical proportions.

Glam

Modern descendant; carries forward the metallic, luxurious, and dramatic sensibility.
