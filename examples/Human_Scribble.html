Human Scribble

the anti-AI aesthetic -- imperfect by design, honest by intention

A deliberate embrace of imperfection, hand-drawn marks, and visible human authorship in an age of algorithmically generated content. Scribbles over logos, doodles on photographs, sketch-like overlays, handwritten margin notes, signature typography. Every wobble and ink blot says "a human made this."

Where polished digital design strives for pixel-perfect alignment and mathematical precision, Human Scribble celebrates the warmth of a shaky hand, the charm of crossed-out corrections, and the personality embedded in someone's actual handwriting.

It is a counter-movement to sterile AI-generated perfection -- asserting that imperfection is not a flaw but a signature of genuine human presence.

Visual Characteristics

the defining traits

The Human Scribble aesthetic is built on a set of visual principles that all point to the same idea: a real person touched this page. Here are its defining characteristics:

Wobbly Lines

Deliberately imperfect lines, borders, and paths that mimic real pen strokes with natural hand tremor.

Arrow Annotations

Hand-drawn arrows with labels pointing to content, like a teacher marking up a student's work.

Doodle Overlays

Stars, hearts, squiggles, and speech bubbles layered on images as if someone doodled on a printed photo.

Crossed-Out Corrections

Visible strikethroughs with handwritten replacements above, showing the thinking process in real time.

Sticky Notes

Post-it style callouts with tape effects, slightly rotated and overlapping content like real desk notes.

Sketchy Underlines

Wavy, hand-drawn underlines under important text -- emphatic, like scribbling in a notebook.

More Visual Traits

  • Organic blob shapes -- containers with irregular border-radius that feel amoeba-like
  • Ink splatter and blot accents -- decorative marks that resemble pen drips on paper
  • Notebook and paper textures -- lined paper, graph paper, kraft backgrounds
  • Slight rotation on elements -- cards tilted 1-3 degrees, casually placed
  • Mixed typography contrast -- clean sans-serif body alongside handwritten headings
  • Warm, lived-in color temperature -- cream backgrounds and real art-supply colors
  • Sketch-like border decorations -- uneven thickness, wobbly corners, deliberate gaps

Typography

the contrast is the message

Human Scribble typography is defined by the contrast between hand-drawn and machine-set text. Clean body text says "this is the content," while handwritten overlays say "a person was here". The juxtaposition is the entire point.

The target is everyday handwriting and quick sketches, not formal calligraphy. Casual, not precious. The kind of marks people leave on paper when thinking out loud.

Key Design Elements

The building blocks of Human Scribble design -- each one a deliberate assertion of human craft over machine precision.

Hand-Drawn Borders & Frames

The defining decorative element. Borders that look sketched by hand rather than rendered by a computer. Wobbly rectangular borders, double-line sketch borders, corner flourishes with small doodle marks, torn-edge effects, and variable stroke width mimicking real pen pressure.

Notice the border on this very card -- imperfect by intention.

Crossed-Out Corrections

Original text crossed out and replaced, just like editing a draft in pen. The humanity perfection    is in showing the process. Visible mistakes decisions convey authenticity. Carets, insertion marks, and scribbled-over text all mimic the look of a hand-edited manuscript.

Sticky Notes & Paper Scraps

Secondary content presented on "stuck-on" elements that break the grid, like real desk clutter with purpose:

Layout Principles

The spatial composition of Human Scribble is intentionally imperfect but carefully designed. Like a notebook page, content does not fill every pixel -- breathing room suggests a human laid this out casually.

  • Warm paper background -- the entire page sits on cream, not stark white
  • Intentionally imperfect alignment -- subtle rotations (0.5-3 degrees) and offsets
  • Layered composition -- doodles and annotations layered over structured content
  • Generous whitespace -- like a notebook page, content breathes
  • Mixed media zones -- clean typography adjacent to handwritten doodles
  • Margin annotations -- notes overlapping content edges
  • Bottom-heavy compositions -- signatures and personal marks anchored at the bottom
Use CSS Grid/Flexbox for structure, then apply slight rotations to individual elements to break the mechanical feel.

Key web techniques: transform: rotate() on cards, organic border-radius values on containers, SVG doodle elements positioned absolutely, and max-width containers (700-900px) for comfortable reading width.

Imagery Guidelines

When sourcing or creating imagery for Human Scribble designs, the core principle is visible human authorship:

  • Layer hand-drawn elements over photography -- add SVG scribbles, arrows, circles, and doodles on top of photos
  • Favor imperfect, candid compositions -- avoid overly polished stock photography
  • Mix media types -- photographs alongside hand-drawn illustrations and sketches
  • Avoid AI-generated imagery -- the entire point is asserting human authorship
  • Scan real sketches -- authentic scanned doodles carry more visual truth than digital equivalents
  • Muted, warm color grading -- lean warm (cream, amber, soft brown) not cool or hyper-saturated
Never use this aesthetic to disguise AI-generated content. The entire philosophical foundation is human authenticity.

Implementation Notes

The Rules of Scribble

  • Warm and inviting, not messy -- visible human presence, not visual disorder
  • Contrast is essential -- without clean body text, the handwritten overlays lose meaning
  • Imperfection must be designed -- rotations 0.5-3 degrees, wobbles gentle, doodles purposeful
  • SVG is your primary tool -- CSS alone cannot produce convincingly hand-drawn lines
  • Accessibility is critical -- handwriting fonts only for headings and short labels, never body text
  • Performance matters -- use inline SVG data URIs for small elements, external files for larger pieces
  • Color restraint -- limit to 2-3 accent colors per view, like a real pen collection
Paradoxically, achieving the "imperfect" look requires careful design choices. Over-rotating or over-scribbling becomes distracting rather than charming. The magic is in subtlety.

Key CSS Techniques

:root { --scribble-paper-light: #FBF7F0; --scribble-ink: #2A2520; --scribble-red-pen: #D45040; } /* Organic blob shape */ .blob { border-radius: 60% 40% 50% 50% / 40% 60% 50% 50%; } /* Casual placement */ .card { transform: rotate(-0.5deg); } /* Highlighter mark */ mark { background: linear-gradient(to bottom, transparent 40%, rgba(255, 224, 102, 0.45) 40%, rgba(255, 224, 102, 0.45) 85%, transparent 85% ); }

Human Scribble is not about being sloppy or careless. It is about making the deliberate choice to show the hand behind the work. In a world increasingly filled with content that could have been made by anyone -- or anything -- the visible mark of a specific human being is the most powerful design statement there is.

Every wobble is a choice. Every mistakerevision is a story. Every ink blot is proof of life.
