Design Aesthetic Reference

☘ Clovercore

~ Happy Clover Days ~

A nature-inspired, hyper-cute aesthetic rooted in late 1990s to early 2000s Japanese stationery culture. Centered on the four-leaf clover as a symbol of luck, happiness, and innocence -- blending organic, earthy greens with glossy, pastel sweetness.

Color Palette

Green-dominant with pink accents. Clover greens for 60-70% of colored elements, pink for 20-30%, other pastels for the remaining 10%. High white-space ratio, warm -- never stark.

✨ Color Approach

Pastel layering for depth without heaviness. Glossy, candy-like finishes via subtle gradients and highlights. Avoid pure black text -- use warm brown-grays instead.

Typography Characteristics

  • ☘ Rounded, friendly sans-serif -- soft terminals, no sharp corners
  • ☘ Slightly playful proportions -- a little bouncy or handwritten in feel
  • ☘ Lightweight to medium weights -- the mood is light and airy
  • ☘ Title case preferred -- decorative capitals, never all-caps
  • ☘ Handwritten accents -- for Engrish phrases and decorative labels
  • ☘ Small text sizes for charm -- echoing stationery memo-pad scale

Layout Principles

~ Nature is my friend ~

Grid & Structure

  • ☘ Centered, single-column primary layout
  • ☘ Card-based "sticker" panels
  • ☘ Generous padding and margins
  • ☘ Rounded containers everywhere (12-24px)
  • ☘ Max width: 800-960px
  • ☘ Soft grid, organic placement

Section Organization

  • ♥ Decorative dividers between sections
  • ♥ Alternating pastel backgrounds
  • ♥ Hierarchy through color, not size
  • ♥ Floating decorative elements
  • ♥ Dense but organized decoration
  • ♥ Clear content hierarchy always
