Design Principles

The 70s Design Philosophy

analog warmth, unapologetic boldness, handcrafted soul

Analog Warmth Over Digital Precision

Every element should feel as though it was printed on textured paper, carved from wood, or woven by hand rather than generated by a computer. The 70s valued the imperfect human touch.

Unapologetic Boldness

The 70s were loud, confident, and expressive. Typography should be oversized, colors should be saturated, and patterns should be layered without hesitation or restraint.

Earth-First With Disco Accents

Ground the design in muted natural tones but allow strategic bursts of gold shimmer, mirror-ball sparkle, or warm metallic glow to keep the energy alive and dancing.

Pattern As Personality

Repeating patterns — stripes, florals, geometrics, paisleys — are core design elements rather than decoration. Pattern communicates the era more than any single element.
