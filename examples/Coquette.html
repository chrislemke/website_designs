les traits visuels

Visual Characteristics

The core design language of Coquette is built on softness, ornamentation, and an intimate sense of dreamy romance.

Satin Bows & Ribbons

Decorative bow shapes used as dividers, icons, and ornamental accents throughout the design.

Lace & Filigree

Delicate lace-pattern overlays and filigree borders framing content panels and images.

Blush-Pink Dominance

A palette anchored in soft pinks, rose, and champagne tones with cream and ivory neutrals.

Pearl & Jewel Accents

Small circular pearl-like elements, cameo shapes, and gemstone-inspired decorative dots.

Soft-Focus & Bloom

Gaussian blur, vignetting, and diffused lighting that create a dreamy, romantic atmosphere.

Pillow-Soft Shapes

Generous border-radius values, rounded cards, and organic bubble-like containers.

les principes

Design Principles

Guiding philosophies that keep every Coquette creation feeling intentional and enchanting.

  • Prioritize Softness Every visual decision should lean soft: soft shadows, soft edges, soft colors. Nothing harsh or angular.
  • Generous White Space Ample breathing room maintains an airy, open composition and prevents visual clutter.
  • Layer Translucent Pinks Build gentle depth by overlapping translucent pinks and creams without heaviness.
  • Intentional Ornamentation Every bow and pearl should feel purposeful — treat ornamentation as charm, never clutter.
  • Whispered Tone Nothing should feel loud, bold, or aggressive. The aesthetic speaks in soft, intimate murmurs.
  • Asymmetric Balance Decorative elements lean gracefully off-center — embrace the beauty of elegant asymmetry.

la mise en page

Layout Principles

Spatial guidelines that preserve the intimate, breathable quality that defines Coquette composition.

Centered & Intimate

Content blocks stay narrow (600–800px max-width) and centered, creating an intimate reading experience akin to a personal letter.

Generous Vertical Rhythm

Ample spacing between sections (60–100px) lets the soft palette breathe and prevents the layout from feeling crowded.

Layered Softness

Cards and panels use multiple layers of soft shadows and translucent pink overlays to build gentle depth without weight.

Ornamental Margins

Decorative flourishes, small bow icons, and pearl-dot dividers fill the space between content sections gracefully.

Image Treatment

All images feel soft-focus with rounded corners, subtle pink-tinted overlays, or vignette borders that maintain the dreamy quality.

Responsive Approach

On mobile, maintain generous padding and the single-column intimate feel. Scale down ornaments gracefully rather than removing them.

les conseils

Do’s & Don’ts

Essential guidelines to keep your Coquette designs feeling authentic and refined.

Do Embrace

  • Use soft, diffused shadows instead of harsh drop shadows
  • Layer translucent pinks to create warmth and depth
  • Include ornamental script typography for decorative accents
  • Round all corners generously (16–32px border-radius)
  • Apply soft-focus or pink-tinted treatments to photography
  • Use pearl-dot patterns and bow motifs as recurring elements
  • Maintain a light, airy composition with abundant white space

Don’t Avoid

  • Sharp geometric angles or hard-edged containers
  • Bold, saturated primary colors that overpower the soft palette
  • Setting all text in script fonts; reserve cursive for small accents
  • Heavy black shadows or dark backgrounds
  • Crowding the layout with too many competing ornaments
  • Industrial or brutalist typefaces
  • Combining Coquette with grunge, cyberpunk, or hard-edged aesthetics
les voisins

Related Aesthetics

Kindred design movements that share sensibilities with Coquette while diverging in tone and temperament.

Rococo

Direct historical ancestor. Coquette borrows its pastel palette, gilded ornament, and asymmetric flourishes from 18th-century French decorative arts.

Baroque

Shares opulence and ornamentation, but Baroque is heavier, darker, and more dramatically theatrical in its approach.

Cottagecore

Both celebrate femininity and softness. Cottagecore is rustic and rural while Coquette is urban and luxurious.

Art Nouveau

Shares flowing organic curves and decorative elegance. Art Nouveau is more botanical and structurally driven.

Coastal Style

Overlaps in soft, light color palettes. Coastal is breezy and nautical rather than romantic and ornate.

Maximalism

Coquette's ornamental density shares maximalist instincts but constrains them within a pastel, feminine register.
