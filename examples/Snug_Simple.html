Design Aesthetic Reference

Design that feels like a warm blanket

Snug Simple is a cozy, human-centered web design aesthetic that prioritizes warmth, approachability, and intentional simplicity. The anti-corporate antidote -- rounded everything, soft pastels, chunky typography, and generous spacing that says "slow down, be comfortable."

Visual characteristics

Born from Scandinavian hygge, indie web design, and the desire for digital spaces that feel like home.

⚭

Pillow-like rounding

Large border-radius everywhere -- cards, buttons, images, and containers all use generous rounding (16-32px), creating a soft, pillow-like feel across the entire interface.

border-radius: 28px Soft edges
🌸

Muted pastel palette

Sage greens, blush pinks, warm creams, soft lavenders, and dusty roses form the primary palette -- never saturated or harsh, always at 30-60% saturation.

Blush pink Sage green Lavender
✎

Chunky bold type

Rounded sans-serif typefaces at large sizes with heavy weights (600-800) convey warmth and friendliness without aggression. Every letterform feels like it is smiling.

Quicksand Nunito
🌄

Generous breathing room

Elements breathe with ample margins (2-4rem) and padding (1.5-3rem). Spacing is a feature, not waste -- the generous whitespace is what makes Snug Simple feel relaxed.

4-6rem gaps line-height: 1.8
🌞

Warm shadows

Subtle box-shadows with warm color tints replace harsh drop shadows. Use rgba() shadows tinted with warm colors -- brown, rose, sage -- rather than neutral gray.

Tinted shadows Gentle depth
🌱

Intentional simplicity

Simple single-column or two-column layouts -- content flows naturally without complex grid systems. Ornamentation limited to soft color blocks, gentle curves, and simple shapes.

Minimal Focused

The core principle

The core principle of Snug Simple is comfort through restraint. Every design decision should ask: "Does this make the user feel more at ease?" If an element creates visual tension, urgency, or complexity, it does not belong.

Typography rules

  • • Rounded sans-serif typefaces only
  • • Weights 500 minimum for body, 600-800 for headings
  • • Body text at 17-18px, never cramped
  • • Line-height 1.7-2.0 for comfortable reading
  • • No uppercase headings -- sentence or title case only
  • • Espresso brown text, never pure black

Layout principles

Complexity is the enemy of coziness -- grid systems are kept minimal, content flows naturally.

  • ▦
    Single or two-column layouts -- content flows naturally without complex grid systems or overwhelming density.
  • ◻
    Centered containers at 1000-1200px max-width, never edge-to-edge. Text paragraphs never exceed 65ch.
  • ▲
    Card-based organization -- information grouped into discrete, rounded, softly contained blocks like sticky notes.
  • ■
    No hard dividers -- sections separated by whitespace and alternating pastel background colors rather than lines.
  • ◑
    Frosted glass navigation -- if navigation is fixed, it uses a soft frosted-glass effect with pastel tint and backdrop-filter.
  • ◉
    Gentle scroll animations -- fade-in and slight upward float on reveal, never bounce or snap. Transitions 0.2-0.4s easing.

Related aesthetics

Design cousins and siblings -- how Snug Simple relates to other cozy visual languages.

Cutecore

Shares rounded forms and pastels; pushes further into kawaii with brighter pinks, while Snug Simple stays muted and restrained.

Danish Pastel

Close sibling with soft pastels and rounded forms; draws more from Scandinavian interior design with checkered patterns and arched shapes.

Cottagecore

Overlapping warmth and comfort; more specifically rural and nature-themed with floral patterns, while Snug Simple is more abstract.

Coquette

Shares softness and pastel pinks but leans romantic with bows and ribbons; Snug Simple is gender-neutral and minimal.

Claymorphism

Shares rounded containers and soft shadows; uses more dramatic inner shadows and inflated 3D effects, while Snug Simple stays flat.

Dopamine Design

Both prioritize friendliness; Dopamine uses saturated bold colors and energy, while Snug Simple deliberately mutes everything for calm.

Implementation notes

The practical details that keep the coziness consistent and intentional.

  • ⚠
    Border-radius is non-negotiable. Sharp corners immediately break the aesthetic. Minimum 12px on everything, 20-28px on cards, 999px on buttons and tags.
  • ⚪
    Avoid pure black and pure white. Use warm off-black (#3D3028) for text and warm off-white (#FFF8F0) for backgrounds. Pure extremes create harshness.
  • ✏
    Typography weight matters enormously. Thin or light weights feel cold and corporate. Use 500 minimum for body, 600-800 for headings. Type should feel chunky.
  • ▢
    Spacing is a feature, not waste. Resist the impulse to tighten margins. The generous whitespace is what makes it feel relaxed. If the page feels "empty," it is working.
  • 🎨
    Pastel saturation must stay low. Colors should feel dusty, muted, and grown-up. If a color looks like it belongs on a birthday cake, desaturate by 20-30%.
  • ⚡
    Interaction design should be gentle. No snappy, aggressive animations. Transitions use 0.2-0.4s easing. Hover effects are subtle lifts or soft color shifts.
  • ♿
    Accessibility matters. Espresso brown (#3D3028) on warm cream (#FFF8F0) achieves approximately 9:1 contrast ratio -- excellent for WCAG AA compliance.
